Paper 1: Submitted 2022 IEEE International Conference on Robotics and Automation (ICRA) (under review)

  • Ma. G., Ross, W., Codd, P. Robotic Laser Orientation Planning with a 3D Data-driven Method (Submitted to ICRA 2022).


    • The demo shows the result of the 2022 ICRA paper (submitted, context will be updated to Arxiv later). The goal of this paper is to design a laser orientation planner to find the optimal 6-DOF laser pose for a specified surgical objective, such as avoiding overcutting laser resection. This initial work develops a dynamic model of laser-tissue interaction by using a 3D data-driven approach. The analytical gradient is derived from scratch for the design of the optimization algorithm. Initial results from simulation validates the feasibility of the proposed theory.


    The second demo shows the simulation result of the ICRA 2022 submission paper. The video displays the trajectory of each point robot agent generated by the proposed orientation planning method. The point robot can be controlled by the gradient field pre-defined by the synthetis obstacle boundary. This enables each point to keep the distance from the obstacle boundary, and move to a "safe region" after the planning procedure.


    The third video illustrates the simulation results with different conditions (initial positions). This indicates that the proposed motion planner work in different initial surface positions, as a validation for the proposed theory.




    Paper 2: 2021 International Conference on Intelligent Robots and Systems (IROS) (accepted)

  • Ma. G., Ross, W., Codd, P. StereoCNC: A Stereovision-guided Robotic Laser System, submitted to 2021 International Conference on Intelligent Robots and Systems (IROS 2021), (Accepted). [pdf]


    • This demo shows the complete setup of stereovision-guided robotic laser system. The CAD models of the complete mechanical system is visualized with VTK toolkit and diffferent coordinates frames are displayed. The major objective of this research is to design an effective system calibration method to register the Stereo sensor to the system frame. This enables the fast 3D reconstruction and error quantification in a unified system.


    This demo illustrates the human-in-the-loop image-guided laser surgery process based on the proposed system calibration method. After optimized sensor frame registration, the image information can be transformed to the system model and the robot can achieve automated vision-guided tissue resection.




    Paper 3: 2021 IEEE International Conference on Robotics and Automation (ICRA) (accepted)

  • Ma, G.*, Oca, S*. (Equal contributed), Zhu, Y., Codd, P. and Buckland, D. A Novel Robotic System for Ultrasound-guided Peripheral Vascular Localization, 2021 International Conference on Robotics and Automation (ICRA 2021). [pdf]


    • This demo introduces the project of "Multi-sensors guided and Robot-assisted Peripheral Vein Localization System". The goal is to design a multi-sensors guided robotic arm effector to achieve vision-based control for 3D peripheral vein localization. This research can inspire the potential topics of automated intravenous insertion.


    This demo illustrates the animation of 3D real-time vein localization. The 3D contours and vessel trajectory are denoted by the colorized point cloud. This indicates the feasibility of using multi-sensors for 3D vein localization for the fully automated level.




    Research project: 3D Vision guided Robotic Laser Research Platform.

    This demo shows the ongoing research of modelling geometric configuration of the optical beam path propagation. This model can be applied to the design of the robotic laser system that can achieve the surface scanning and laser-tissue resection. This demo illustrates the simulation of the optimal path with different laser pose and the resulted beam paths formulate a unique configuration in 3D space.


    Following the last video, this demo displays geometry laser beam propagation model based on different configurations of the 2-DOF galvonometer (X-Y mirror for controlling the orientation of the laser beam).
